You are doing three things at once inside the OGR pipeline. Reading handwriting. Preserving the way the page is laid out. Catching the underlines, highlights, and strikes. Most teams treat those as one model problem. They are not. They are three problems stacked on top of each other, and each one belongs on a different layer.

60%

Traditional code and database work

Layout preservation, notebook organization, the subscription logic, the sync layer.

30%

Rule-based logic

The classifier that decides this mark is an underline and that one is a strikethrough. The per-notebook styling presets. The correction feedback loop, the part that decides which corrections to trust.

10%

Real AI

The character and word recognition itself. That is the only layer where a model genuinely earns its cost.

When the layers blur, the user corrections stop compounding the way they should, the roadmap slows down, and the team ships slower than the headcount would predict. We call this figuring-out-where-each-problem-lives computational orchestration.
